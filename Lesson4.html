Types of Attacks

Social Engineering Phishing Viruses Malware and Ransomware

What is Social Engineering?

Social Engineering is the practice of manipulating employees in an organization to bypass security measures and obtain access to private information.

Social Engineering uses intimidation, authority, familiarity, trust, and urgency to take advantage of others in order to further the hackers personal agenda.

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of social engineering where an attacker sends a fraudulent message designed to trick a person into revealing sensitive information to the attacker.

What is a Virus?

A Virus is a type of malicious software, or malware, that spreads between computers and causes damage to data and software. They aim to disrupt systems, cause major operational issues, and result in data loss and leakage.

What is Malware? Ransomware?

Malware is software that is specifically designed to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorized access to a computer system.

Ransomware is a form of malware which locks users out of their systems while cutting off access to their files. The attacker then demands a ransom and in return they will return any information that was stolen.
